Frequently Asked Questions

Once my data is written in the blockchain, can I change it ?

Your access data like your fingerprint or VoiceID are saved on your device. What is saved in the blockchain is an encrypted representation of this data and once you remove a device from your circle of trust, the access token becomes invalid and can't be used to access your account anymore.

With which OS and devices is Esna Compatible ?

Esna works on any device and cross operating-systems and is compatible with the main web browsers (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Opera and Edge), it doesn't require the installation of additional plugins or software.

Do you store the data on any servers or in the cloud ?

No. Users access data are not stored in any physical servers nor in the cloud. Data are solely saved on users devices at their only discretion. The keys used to encrypt the data are stored on the device secure-enclave which offers the highest level of security possible.

Does Esna have access to user identity information ?

Esna doesn’t detain any identity informations or user data. Users have control over their identity and can share certain pieces of information like email address or phone number with 3rd parties.

What is the blockchain ?

The blockchain is a type of distributed ledger or decentralised database that keeps records of digital transactions. Rather than having a central administrator like a traditional database, (think banks, governments & accountants), a distributed ledger has a network of replicated databases, synchronised via the internet and visible to anyone within the network.

How do you use the blockchain for authentication?

Esna completely ditches the traditional client-server authentication model. It uses a new protocol known as the "proof-of-identity" that relies on a computer network to verify and authenticate a user before giving them access to a certain account.

Esna Timeline

Esna Project was launched in 2018 in response to the increasingly common IT security breaches which led to losses of over $3.5 billion USD in the past 3 years in the US alone.

2018 - FedEx security breach

In February 2018, it was discovered that 119,000 scanned documents were left publicly accessible on an Amazon S3 server. These documents included passports and photo IDs of FedEx customers, as well as accompanying attached forms.

2017 - Equifax Data Breach

Equifax disclosed that 145.5 million Social Security numbers were compromised in 2017 — representing more than 99 percent of all of the affected consumers. More than 200,000 credit card details were stolen, as well as users passports, driver's licenses, taxpayer ID cards and military IDs — that consumers uploaded to Equifax.

2017 - OneLogin security breach

OneLogin user database is breached exposing estimated over 12 million of users data, including the ability to decrypt their information and access to their stored passwords acorss all devices.

2016 - Yahoo security breach

Yahoo! reported over 1 billion user records breached in 2013 that remained exposed for 3 years

2016 - U.S. Department of Justice

U.S. Department of Justice reported that hackers released data on 10,000 Department of Homeland Security employees one day, and then released data on 20,000 FBI employees the next day. Information stolen included names, titles, phone numbers, e-mail addresses and Social security numbers.

2016 - Healthcare data breach

21st Century Oncology, a Fort Myers-based company offering cancer care services, revealed in a statement on their website that 2.2 million patients may have had personal information stolen when the company’s system was breached in October 2015.
